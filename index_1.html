The Four Indicators

Cash Flow Affordability (CFA) Debt Service Coverage Ratio: Projects if borrower can cover loan payment based on 90-day cash flow analysis. Primary predictor of repayment capacity.
40%
DSCR = (AvgDailyIncome - AvgDailyExpenses) × LoanTerm / LoanAmount
  • DSCR ≥ 1.5 + Coverage 90+ days Strong affordability: Projected surplus covers 150%+ of loan. Low default probability (~5%). Eligible for Tier 3 ($200, 90-day term). 70-100
  • DSCR 1.2-1.49 + Coverage 60-89 days Adequate affordability: Projected surplus covers 120-149% of loan. Moderate default risk (~9%). Eligible for Tier 2 ($150, 60-day term). 60-69
  • DSCR 1.0-1.19 + Coverage 45-59 days Marginal affordability: Projected surplus barely covers loan. Higher default risk (~14%). Eligible for Tier 1 ($100, 45-day term) only. 50-59
  • DSCR < 1.0 or Coverage <45 days Insufficient affordability: Cannot reliably cover loan payment. High default probability (>20%). Application denied. 0-49
Transaction Velocity & Consistency Data Reliability Score: Measures account maturity and transaction patterns. More history = better predictions = lower model risk.
25%
DataReliability = AccountAge × TransactionFrequency × Consistency
  • 6+ months + 50+ tx/month + consistent High data reliability: Mature account with dense, consistent transaction history. Model predictions highly accurate. Supports all tier applications. 70-100
  • 3-6 months + 30-49 tx/month Moderate data reliability: Established account with sufficient history. Model predictions reasonably accurate. Supports Tier 1-2 applications. 60-69
  • 1-3 months + 15-29 tx/month Limited data reliability: New account with minimal history. Model predictions less certain. May support Tier 1 with strong other indicators. 50-59
  • <1 month or <15 tx/month Insufficient data: Cannot reliably predict behavior. High model uncertainty. Recommend waiting for more account history. 0-49
Deposit / Employment Stability Income Predictability Score: Measures income source quality and stability. Stable income = predictable repayment capacity.
20%
IncomeStability = IncomeSource × (1 - CoefficientOfVariation)
  • Verified payroll + CV ≤15% Excellent income stability: Verified employment with low income variance. Coefficient of Variation ≤15% indicates highly predictable cash flow for loan term. 70-100
  • Regular deposits + CV 15-25% Good income stability: Consistent deposits (bi-weekly/monthly) with acceptable variance. Income predictable enough for Tier 1-2 loan terms. 60-69
  • Irregular deposits + CV 25-40% Moderate income stability: Variable income pattern (gig economy, freelance). Higher uncertainty in projecting repayment capacity. Tier 1 only. 50-59
  • No pattern or CV >40% Poor income stability: No identifiable income pattern or extreme variance. Cannot project future income with confidence. Application denied. 0-49
Behavior / Derogatory Transactions Credit Behavior Risk Score: Identifies financial stress signals. Derogatory events are leading indicators of default risk.
15%
BehaviorScore = 100 - (DerogEvents × EventSeverityWeight)
  • Zero derogatory events (90 days) Excellent credit behavior: No NSF, ACH returns, overdrafts, or stop payments. Indicates strong financial management and low default risk. 70-100
  • 1 minor event (NSF or overdraft) Acceptable credit behavior: Single isolated incident. May indicate temporary cash flow issue. Monitor but approvable for all tiers. 60-69
  • 2-3 events or 1 stop payment Elevated credit risk: Pattern of financial stress emerging. Stop payments indicate intentional non-payment. Limit to Tier 1 only. 50-59
  • 4+ events or payday loan activity High credit risk: Chronic financial distress or debt cycle indicators. Payday loan usage = 3x higher default probability. Application denied. 0-49
CFRS = (Affordability × 0.40) + (Transaction Velocity × 0.25) + (Deposit Stability × 0.20) + (Behavior × 0.15)

Final Recommendations

DO - Priority Actions

  • Enforce minimum CFRS 60 (DSCR ≥1.0)
  • Require DSCR ≥1.5 for Tier 3 ($200)
  • Focus on tier graduation to improve DSCR
  • Monitor CV% for income stability
  • Track derogatory events as leading indicator

DON'T - Avoid

  • Lower CFRS below 60 (DSCR <1.0 = negative cash flow)
  • Approve Tier 3 with CFRS <70 or DSCR <1.5
  • Ignore Tier 1 default >14% alerts
  • Approve accounts <2 months old for Tier 2+
  • Accept CV >25% without payroll verification

State Rollout Strategy

Rollout Advantages by Phase

Phase 1-2 (Full Access): Build complete funnel T1→T2→T3. Higher initial losses but critical for customer LTV.
Phase 3 (Limited): Start at T2 = better initial unit economics (+$0.70 vs -$2.53). Skip loss-leader phase.
Phase 4 (Restricted): Premium only = $8.48/loan profit. No acquisition losses. Target established borrowers.
